II. Related Work

Retrieval-based methods match images against large geo-tagged databases using cross-view or correlation-aware representations [1], [2], while classification approaches discretize the Earth into regions and predict a class label [3]. Hybrid models combine both [4]. Despite strong performance, these methods produce deterministic predictions and fail to capture ambiguity, as visually similar scenes can correspond to distant geographic locations.

Semantic signals have been introduced to improve generalization. Models such as StreetCLIP and PIGEON leverage synthetic captions or metadata [4], [5], but remain discriminative and output point estimates.

Generative approaches model geolocation as a spatial distribution. Dufour et al. [6] use diffusion and flow matching, while LocDiff [7] applies latent diffusion. These methods capture spatial ambiguity but are designed for global-scale localization on spherical manifolds and rely solely on visual conditioning.

In this work, we revisit flow matching-based geolocalization in a local setting focused on Switzerland. Instead of modeling trajectories on the Earth sphere \(S^2\), we study a simpler Euclidean formulation in \(\mathbb{R}^2\), adapted to the limited geographic region considered. We additionally explore other conditioning signals, including image captions and canton-level classifier priors, to evaluate whether they can improve flow matching-based geolocalization.
