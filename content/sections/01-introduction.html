I. Introduction

Image geolocalization asks a simple but difficult question: where was this image taken? In our setting, the output is a latitude and longitude inside Switzerland, inferred from street-level visual evidence such as roads, landscape, mountains, architecture, signs, and scene context.

The Switzerland subset of OpenStreetView-5M contains 19,917 training images, 1,991 validation images, and 1,993 test images. This local scope makes the task small enough to study carefully, while still leaving hard ambiguities between visually similar roads, villages, railway scenes, and mountain regions.

A key goal is to go beyond deterministic point prediction. When an image is ambiguous, the model should express uncertainty as a spatial distribution rather than pretending that a single coordinate is fully certain.
