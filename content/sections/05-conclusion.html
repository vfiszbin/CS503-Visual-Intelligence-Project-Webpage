V. Conclusion and Limitations

Text Was Not Enough

Caption and prompting variants did not improve performance in the final experiments.

Geometry Mattered Most

The S2-to-R2 transition was the clearest architectural gain for Switzerland-only geolocalization.

Priors Helped Regions

Classifier priors improved canton accuracy from 54.89% to 58.96% while keeping distance at 13.3 km.

The main lesson is that the best model comes from matching the geometry and priors to the task. The local R2 formulation should not be read as universally better than spherical modeling; it works here because the prediction space is restricted to Switzerland.

Important limitations remain. The model inherits the CH-only inductive bias, may not generalize globally, and can struggle with ambiguous or generic scenes. The progress presentation also highlights cigar-shaped distributions and data-bias effects as failure modes worth investigating further.

Future work should test robustness, data augmentation, stronger image encoders such as DINO, better classifier priors, and explicit uncertainty calibration for ambiguous images.
